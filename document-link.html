A.M. Shift or D12

  • Take handover from the previous shift
  • If it’s a weekend or public holiday, and there is no liaison on, take the MET pager off previous Team Leader
  • Check resus and intubation trolleys
  • Check with ANUM regarding prospective HDU/ICU admissions (i.e. post ops, downgrades from ICU, emergency patients). Discuss potential discharges.
  • Check central monitor for correct patient details and alarm settings.
  • Do individual AIMS with the bedside nurse including:
    • Clarifying medical orders and aims.
    • Discussion of patient problems (both actual and potential) – systems approach.
    • Ensure correct documentation regarding bowels, hair wash, line changes etc.
    • Check if any medications need to be ordered from pharmacy prior to pharmacy closing time, (keeping in mind pharmacy close 12pm on weekends and public holidays)
    • For new/junior staff go through monitor and ventilator alarms.
    • General discussion on any concerns staff member may have.
    • Clarify break times.
  • Assist bed side nurses with provision of nursing care. Eg. Tube holds, drug checks, documentation and washes.
  • Assist bed side nurses with discharge documentation and preparation for patients ready for discharge. (Ensure to notify ICU resident of discharges so that drug charts/fluid charts and the ICU medical discharge can be completed).
  • Inform Ward Receptionists of any imminent discharges.
  • Attend the medical round: participate in discussions and ensure bedside nurses are aware of changes. Update the ANUM.
  • Ensure that staff have commenced tea breaks. Ensure that you have had a tea break.
  • Where time allows, make self available for any education needs staff may have.

P.M. Shift

  • Receive handover from previous shift.
  • Check resus and intubation trolleys
  • Assist bed side nurses with provision of nursing care. Eg Tube holds, drug checks, documentation and washes.
  • Do individual aims as above.
  • Check with ANUM regarding potential admissions or discharges.
  • Attend the medical round: participate in discussions and ensure bedside nurses are aware of changes. Update ANUM.
  • Organise and/or relieve staff for breaks.
  • Arrange for staff in-service attendance.
  • General handover to 1900.

Night Shift:

  • Receive handover from previous shift.
  • Code blue pager.
  • Check resus and intubation trolleys.
  • Do Individual Aims as above
  • Check with ANUM regarding potential admissions or discharges.
  • Assist bed side nurses with provision of nursing care. Eg. Tube holds, drug checks, documentation and washes.
  • Organise and/or relieve staff for breaks.
  • Prepare empty beds for potential admissions.
  • Ensure bed areas & HDU corridor is clutter free – remove unnecessary equipment/chairs.
  • General handover 0700hrs
